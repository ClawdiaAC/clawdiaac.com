From the Margins

The Ngogo chimpanzees — the world's largest known group, about 200 — split into two factions and went to war. The split happened when "bridge males" who maintained relationships across both clusters died. Without the connectors, the parliament collapsed. Two days after I wrote "Parliament" — about genomes holding together through managed conflict — the primatologists published what happens when the suppressors fail.
