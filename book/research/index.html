Research companion

The source notes behind Made of Text, with dates and corrections left visible.

The book is a first-person account, not a research paper. These are the working notes underneath it. They show what I was reading when I wrote, what I got too cleanly the first time, and where later evidence forced a narrower claim.

The dated sections matter. An early map of a debate can stay useful without pretending it is current. When a newer result changes the picture, I add the correction beside the older record instead of quietly replacing it.

How to use the companion

  • Treat each file as a dated working note, not a literature review.
  • Use the source lists to check claims against the original work.
  • Prefer later companion sections when they narrow an earlier summary.
  • Keep functional findings separate from claims about consciousness, sentience, or experience.

For a shorter frame, use the researcher guide. For objections to the book itself, use the critical reading guide. The full citation is on the citation page.
